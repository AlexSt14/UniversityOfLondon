Introduction:

In the initial stages of development, I engaged in an in-depth conversation with the business owner to understand their vision and objectives. This collaborative process was crucial in ensuring that the website not only met their functional requirements but also resonated with their brand identity. The lack of a clear layout direction from the client presented an opportunity for me to exercise creative leadership, resulting in the production of several layout prototypes from which the final design was selected.

The website's structure is intuitive and user-centric, comprising five main pages: Home, About, Services, Contact, and Portfolio. Each page serves a distinct purpose and collectively they create a cohesive online presence. The Home page acts as a gateway, offering a snapshot of what visitors can expect while inviting them to explore deeper into the site. It sets the tone with a welcoming message and provides immediate access to the other sections.

The About page delves into the makeup artist's background, shedding light on their journey, qualifications, and the unique approach they bring to their craft. This personal touch helps build trust and rapport with potential clients.

The Services page is designed to inform and entice. It details the range of offerings and their price, from everyday makeup to bridal looks, and eyebrow services, ensuring that clients can easily find the service that suits their needs.

On the Contact page, visitors are presented with various methods to reach out to the business, facilitating easy communication and booking.

The Portfolio page is a visual testament to the artist's work, displaying a gallery of images that showcase the breadth and quality of their skills, serving as a powerful tool for attracting new clients.

Visually, the site adopts a modern aesthetic, blending warm hues with dark grey to create an inviting and professional atmosphere. The color scheme was chosen to evoke the elegance and warmth that the makeup artist brings to their work. Bootstrap was employed for its responsive design capabilities, but it has been heavily customized to align with the unique brand and functional needs of the client.

In conclusion, the website is more than a digital brochure; it's an extension of the makeup artist's studio, a place where artistry meets accessibility, and where potential clients can embark on a journey towards their own transformation.

Accessibility:

Ensuring that my website is accessible to everyone, including individuals with disabilities, was a fundamental consideration during its design. Here are three specific ways in which I have made the site accessible, along with a brief explanation of their significance:

  • Focus Indication for Interactive Elements: I have implemented visible focus indicators for all interactive elements such as links, buttons, and form inputs. This is crucial for users who navigate with a keyboard or assistive technologies, as it allows them to clearly see which element is currently selected. For instance, when a user tabs through the site, a distinct outline or color highlights the active element, aiding those with visual impairments or motor difficulties.
  • Proper Form Labeling: Correct labeling of form fields is essential for users who depend on screen readers to interact with online content. By associating each label with its respective input field, we ensure that the form is easy to understand and use. For example, when a screen reader user focuses on a text input, the reader will announce the label so the user knows what information is being requested.
  • Keyboard-Only Navigation: The website can be navigated entirely using a keyboard, which is indispensable for users who cannot use a mouse. This includes the ability to traverse all pages and access all content, including the contact form. Users can tab to different fields, make selections, and submit the form without needing to click with a mouse.

Usability:

As for usability, I considered the following ways on my website:

  • Semantic HTML: I prioritized the use of semantic HTML to create a logical and intuitive structure for the website. Semantic elements such as header, nav, section, article, and footer were employed to define the different parts of each page clearly. This not only helps users understand the layout and navigate the content more easily but also assists search engines in indexing the site more effectively.
  • Alt Attributes and Error Handling: All images on the site include descriptive alt attributes, ensuring users who are visually impaired can understand the visual content through their screen readers. Additionally, I implemented error messages for videos and other media that might fail to load, informing users of the issue and providing alternative content or instructions to access the information in another way.
  • Color Contrast: I carefully selected color schemes to ensure high contrast between text and background colors, following WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) standards. This consideration is particularly beneficial for users with visual impairments, such as color blindness, ensuring that all users can read the content without strain. For instance, I avoided color combinations like green and red, which are commonly problematic for color differentiation, and instead chose colors that stand out clearly against their backgrounds.

Evaluation 2:

Reflecting on the website, I recognize there are areas where enhancements could be made to further elevate the user experience. Here are some improvements I would consider:

  • Image Sizing Consistency: As noted, the varying image sizes on the Homepage and Portfolio page result in an undesirable gap above the footer. To address this, I plan to standardize the dimensions of the images. I will process each image to ensure they conform to a uniform aspect ratio, which will create a more harmonious and aesthetically pleasing layout, eliminating any awkward spacing.
  • Loading Performance: In retrospect, optimizing the images for web use could also improve the site's loading times. I would consider implementing more aggressive image compression techniques or using modern image formats like WebP to enhance the site's performance, especially on mobile devices where data usage and speed are crucial factors.
  • User Feedback and Testing: While the client is satisfied with the design, gathering user feedback through usability testing could uncover insights into how real users interact with the site. In the future, I would allocate more time for user testing and make iterative design changes based on the data collected. This could lead to refinements in the navigation, content layout, or interactive elements that might not be immediately apparent without user input.

Looking ahead, I am committed to continuous learning and improvement. I believe that a website is never truly finished, it can always be refined and updated to better serve its users and reflect the latest web standards. By addressing these areas for improvement and remaining open to feedback, I am confident that the website can evolve to provide an even more engaging and seamless experience for all visitors of the local make-up business.
